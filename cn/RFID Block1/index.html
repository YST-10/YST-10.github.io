Lecture 1: RFID Introduction and Overview

1. DEFINITION OF RFID

What is RFID?

  • RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) is a technology that uses radio frequency waves to transfer energy and data between a reader and a movable item to identify, categorize, track, …
    RFID是一种通过射频波在阅读器和可移动物品之间传输能量和数据来识别、分类、跟踪……的技术
  • The item can be an object, a person, an animal, etc.
    物品可以是一个物体、一个人、一个动物等等
  • Identification system consists of chip-based RFID tags, RFID readers, and software
    识别系统由基于芯片的RFID标签、RFID读取器和软件组成，
  • RFID is fast, reliable, and does not require physical sight or contact between reader/scanner and the tag.
    RFID快速、可靠，不需要读取器/扫描仪与标签之间的物理视觉或接触。
  • RFID, dates back to the invention of RADAR, where, during the Second World War, fighter pilots cleverly maneuvered their planes to be remotely identified by friendly radar operators, who distinguished them from their foes (Identify Friend or Foe). RFID，可以追溯到雷达的发明，在第二次世界大战期间，战斗机飞行员巧妙地驾驶他们的飞机被友好的雷达操作员远程识别，他们将他们与敌人区分开来（识别朋友或敌人）。

2. AUTOMATIC IDENTIFICATION SYSTEMS

Identification of Things! —>Internet of Things

(1) Automatic Identification Technology

  • Human identification 人体识别

    • The earliest identification technology originated in humans. Humans use their own perceptual system to identify external physical objects effectively, including visual, auditory, tactile, taste and so on. 最早的识别技术起源于人类。人类利用自己的感知系统来有效地识别外部的物理物体，包括视觉、听觉、触觉、味觉等
    • The cost is too high

  • Automatic identification 自动识别

    • The essence of the recognition technique is to distinguish and identify the physical objects using their identifiable features. 识别技术的本质是利用其可识别的特征来区分和识别物理物体。

(2) Biometric Procedures

  • Biometrics is defined as the science of counting and (body) measurement procedures involving living beings.
    生物识别学被定义为涉及生物的计数和（身体）测量程序的科学。

  • In the context of identification systems, biometry is the general term for all procedures that identify people by comparing unmistakable and individual physical characteristics.
    在识别系统的背景下，生物测量学是所有通过比较明确的和个人的身体特征来识别人的程序的通用称。

  • In practice, there are various biometric procedures:

    • Fingerprinting procedures
    • Voice identification
    • Face recognition
    • Retina identification
    • Heartbeat recognition technology

<1> Fingerprint Identification 指纹识别

  • 定义：Fingerprint identification uses the uniqueness and stability of fingerprint to verify user identity by comparing the collected fingerprints with the pre-sampled fingerprints. 指纹识别利用指纹的唯一性和稳定性，将采集到的指纹与预采样的指纹进行比较，验证用户身份。

  • Stability

    • High accuracy of identification

    • Low error rate

    • Each fingerprint has multiple feature points 每个指纹都有多个特征点

    • Each feature point has 5 to 7 features 每个特征点有5-7个特征

    • Ten fingers can produce at least 4900 independent features that can be measured

      10个手指可以产生至少4900个可以测量的独立特征

  • High speed

  • Fingerprint match can be completed in 1 to 2 seconds

  • Security issues

    • Fingerprints are easy to leave over 指纹很容易留下
    • Fingerprint replication is getting easier 指纹复制变得越来越容易

  • Miniaturization 小型化

  • Intelligent

  • Low price

  • …

<2> Speech Recognition 语音识别

  • Speech recognition uses the difference between each person’s tone and timbre to identify different people. 语音识别使用每个人的音调和音色之间的差异来识别不同的人。

  • Technical Features
    • Easy to use, and easily accepted by the user 使用方便，易被用户接受
    • Cheap sound equipment, and not involve user’s privacy 便宜的音响设备，不涉及用户的隐私
    • Due to the lack of international standards, there are some difficulties in promoting 由于缺乏国际标准，在推广上存在一些困难

<3> Face Recognition 面部识别

  • Face recognition is based on human face features. It performs identity authentication by analyzing the input face image.
    人脸识别是基于人脸特征的。它通过对输入的人脸图像的分析来进行身份验证。

  • Technical Features
    • Surroundings, hair ornaments, age can affect the recognition rate 环境、发饰、年龄等都会影响识别率
    • Fast recognition speed, not easy to be aware of it 识别速度快，不容易察觉

<4> Other Biometric Procedures

  • Retina recognition technology: distinguish each person by analyzing the vascular pattern on the retina.
    视网膜识别技术：通过分析视网膜上的血管模式来区分每个人。

  • Heartbeat recognition technology: identify the user identity by collecting heartbeat signal pulse through a specific instrument and then identify the inherent characteristics of each person heartbeat. 心跳识别技术：通过通过特定的仪器采集心跳信号脉冲来识别用户身份，然后识别每个人心跳的固有特征。

(3)Bar Codes 条形码

  • 定义：The barcode is a binary code comprising a field of bars and gaps arranged in a parallel configuration 条形码是一种二进制代码，由平行配置的条和间隙组成

  • The most popular barcode by some margin is the EAN code (European Article Number)

    • Today, the UPC represents a subset of the EAN code, and is therefore compatible with it.兼容

<1> 1D Barcode Recognition

  • 定义：1D barcode is a combination of lines and blanks in accordance with certain coding rules to represent certain information.
    一维条形码是根据一定的编码规则进行的行和空白的组合来表示一定的信息。

  • 1D barcode only express information in the horizontal direction, and in the vertical direction does not express any information. It usually retains a certain height to facilitate the alignment of the reader.一维条码只表达在水平方向上的信息，而在垂直方向上不表达任何信息。它通常保持一定的高度，以方便读者的对齐。

  • Limited storage capacity, need to combine with the database.存储容量有限，需要与数据库结合使用。

  • The barcode size is relatively large, the space utilization is low.条码尺寸相对较大，空间利用率较低。

  • Fault tolerance is poor, cannot restore the information after the corruption.容错能力较差，损坏后无法恢复信息。

<2> 2D Code Recognition

  • 定义：2D code uses black and white graphics on the two-dimensional plane to record data, these geometric patterns express specific information through a certain regulation of distribution . 二维代码使用二维平面上的黑白图形来记录数据，这些几何图形通过一定的分布规律来表达特定的信息。

  • Positioning point technology: 2D code usually has three positioning points for the reader to identify, because of these positioning points, 2D code can be identified no matter what direction to read. 定位点技术：二维码通常有三个定位点供读者识别，由于这些定位点，无论阅读方向如何，都可以识别出二维码。

  • Fault tolerance: the information can be correctly restored when it does not recognize all of the 2D code, or if the 2D code is defaced. 容错：当信息不能识别所有二维码或损坏二维码时，可以正确恢复信息。

  • Large storage capacity. Up to 32KB. 存储容量大。高达32KB。

  • High information density. Can store text, sound, pictures and other information.较高的信息密度。可存储文字、声音、图片等信息。

  • Powerful error correction capability. 2D code can still be identified in the case of 50% defacement. 具有强大的纠错能力。在50%损坏的情况下，仍然可以识别二维代码。

  • Support for encryption. Multiple security features.支持加密。多个安全功能。

(4) Card Technologies

  • Magnetic cards: containing either a magnetic strip or a magnetic object in the card, encoded with digital data
    磁卡：在卡中包含磁条或磁性物体，用数字数据编码

  • Smart cards: credit card-sized pieces of plastic containing a data storage system. Two type: memory cards and microprocessor cards 智能卡：信用卡大小的塑料片，其中包含数据存储系统。两种类型：存储卡和微处理器卡

  • Optical cards 光卡

(5) Summary of Automatic Identification Technologies

<1> RFID vs Bar Codes

  • Three advantages of using RFID compared to bar code scanning are:
    • More efficiency: you can scan multiple items at once 更有效率：你可以扫描多个项目一次
    • More durability: tags can handle exposure to weather conditions like sun and rain 更耐用：标签可以处理暴露在天气条件下，如太阳和雨
    • More security: you can encrypt RFID tags so only your reader can get the data 更安全：你可以加密RFID标签只有你的读者可以得到数据

<2> RFID vs NFC

  • Near-field communication (NFC) is a subset of the RFID technology family. The big difference is that NFC is used for two-way communication. Instead of the scanner receiving or sending data, both ends can receive and send information.
    近场通信（NFC）是RFID技术家族的一个子集。最大的区别在于NFC是用于双向通信的。两端都可以接收和发送数据，而不是由扫描仪接收或发送数据。

4. HISTORY OF RFID

(1) RFID Overview

  • RFID has been around for over 80 years and has evolved into one of the most powerful technologies for sample tracking and asset management.
    RFID 技术已经存在超过 80 年，发展成为样品跟踪和资产管理中最强大的技术之一。
  • RFID systems can include multiple tags (antennae paired with a microchip) that can send out longwave signals, either actively or in response to a signal emitted by a scanning transmitter.
    RFID 系统可以包括多个标签（天线配有微芯片），可以发送长波信号，可以主动发送信号，也可以响应扫描发射器发出的信号。
  • These tags have revolutionized the industrial sector and biomedical labs and allowed entire sample inventories to be tracked in real-time, without ever removing them from storage.
    这些标签已经彻底改变了工业部门和生物医学实验室，并允许整个样品库存实时跟踪，而无需将其从存储中移出。

(2) WWII Technology

  • RFID 早在二战时期就已经开始了，飞机使用这项技术被识别为“友军还是敌军”。

(3)Setting Standards

  • 1999 年，麻省理工学院科学家成立了 Auto-ID 中心，以标准化 RFID 编码信息格式。
  • 2003 年成立了 EPCGlobal 系统，以进一步标准化 RFID，根据沃尔玛、美国国防部和吉列等公司的要求。

(4) RFID Today

  • RFID 现在被广泛应用于几乎每个领域，从生物医学实验室到航空航天、工程和物流。

  • 使用碳纳米管carbon nanotubes 进一步降低了成本。

  • As RFID technology continues to evolve, it will become cheaper and more user-friendly, ensuring inventories and workflows are well-managed and up to date.
    随着 RFID 技术的不断发展，它将变得更加便宜和用户友好，确保库存和工作流程得到及时和有效的管理。

7. DEVELOPMENT TRENDS IN RFID

(1) Energy Acquisition Mode 能量采集模式

  • Traditional wired transmission: obviously no longer feasible 传统有线传输：显然不再可行

  • Battery-powered: increase the weight and volume, the battery needs to be changed regularly 电池供电：增加重量和体积，电池需要定期更换

  • Wireless charging: not yet reached the practical use 无线充电：尚未达到实际使用

  • Backscatter: time of endurance is prolonged indefinitely, small node size 后向散射：续航时间无限延长，节点大小

    • Disadvantages: rely on the reader, one to many centralized communication 缺点：依赖阅读器，一多集中通信

  • Energy acquisition mode: Design and implement innovative energy harvesting ways to support communication between systems, in order to fully expand the scope and deployment scale of RFID applications.
    能源采集模式：设计并实现创新的能源采集方式，以支持系统之间的通信，以充分扩大RFID应用的范围和部署规模。

  • Ambient backscatter: Create new types of network communication by leveraging radio signals (such as television and cellular network base station signals) that are prevalent in the environment. 环境后向散射：通过利用环境中普遍存在的无线电信号（如电视和蜂窝网络基站信号），创建新的网络通信类型。

(2) Support Point-to-Point Communications

  • Support point-to-point communication: Design and implement innovative channel awareness technologies that enable RFID devices to establish networks and implement point-to-point communications in a passive way to support multiple distributed applications. 支持点对点通信：设计和实现创新的信道感知技术，使RFID设备能够建立网络，并以一种被动的方式实现点对点通信，以支持多个分布式应用程序。

(3) Combine with Sensors

Combine with Sensors: Integrating existing micro-sensor devices, combined with multimode sensors, provides a richer application model based on backscatter technology. 与传感器相结合：集成现有的微传感器设备，结合多模传感器，提供了一个基于后向散射技术的更丰富的应用模型。

    在物联网中，RFID 使被动环境中的物理对象实现了“被动智能”，为“物物连接”提供了基本保障。

(1) Encoding RFID Tags 编码 RFID 标签

  • Encoding RFID tags is a two-step process: 编码 RFID 标签是一个两步的过程：
    • Select the identification scheme 选择识别方案
    • Attach those identities to the RFID tags 将这些标识附加到 RFID 标签上

<1> Deciding on an Item-Numbering Scheme 决定物品编号方案

  • Identification : is the act of recognizing the identity of an object or item.
    识别是识别物体或物品的身份的行为。

5. READERS

(1) Readers

  • 定义：RFID readers, also called interrogators, are used to recognize the presence of nearby RFID tags.
    RFID 读写器，也称为询问器，用于识别附近 RFID 标签的存在。
  • An RFID reader transmits RF energy through one or more antennas. RFID 读写器通过一个或多个天线向外传输射频能量。
  • An antenna in a nearby tag picks up this energy, and the tag then converts it into electrical energy via induction.
    附近的标签中的天线接收到这个能量，然后标签通过感应将其转换为电能。
  • This electrical energy is sufficient to power the semiconductor chip attached to the tag antenna, which stores the tag’s identity. 这种电能足以为连接到标签天线上的半导体芯片供电，该芯片存储了标签的身份信息。
  • The tag then sends the identity back to the reader by raising and lowering the resistance of the antenna in a kind of Morse code. 然后，标签通过提高和降低天线的电阻来以一种摩尔斯电码的方式将身份信息发送回读写器。

(2) Readers Subsystems

  1. Reader Application Programming Interface (API): The reader API allows programs to register for and capture RFID tag read events. It also provides capabilities to configure, monitor, and otherwise manage the reader. 读写器应用程序编程接口（API）：读写器 API 允许程序注册并捕获 RFID 标签读取事件。它还提供了配置、监控和其他管理读写器的功能。
  2. Communications: Readers are edge devices, and like any other RFID device, they are connected to the overall edge network. The communications component handles the networking functions.
    通信：读写器是边缘设备，与任何其他 RFID 设备一样，它们连接到整体边缘网络。通信组件处理网络功能。
  3. Event management: When a reader sees a tag, we call this an observation. An observation that differs from previous observations is called an event. The analysis of observations is called event filtering. Event management defines what kinds of observations are considered events and determines which events are interesting enough to merit being either put in a report or sent immediately to an external application on the network.
    事件管理：当读写器看到一个标签时，我们称之为一个观察。与先前观察不同的观察被称为事件。对观察结果的分析称为事件过滤。事件管理定义了哪些类型的观察被视为事件，并确定哪些事件足够有趣，值得放入报告中或立即发送到网络上的外部应用程序。
  4. Antenna subsystem: The antenna subsystem consists of one or more antennas and the supporting interfaces and logic that enable RFID readers to interrogate RFID tags.
    天线子系统：天线子系统由能使 RFID 读写器能够对 RFID 标签进行询问的一个或多个天线以及支持的接口和逻辑组成。

  • The reader adapter layer encapsulates the proprietary reader interfaces so that they don’t come in contact with the application developer. 读写器适配器层封装了专有的读写器接口，以使其不与应用程序开发人员接触。

    读写器的RFID扫描被视为“原始”，需要进一步处理才能得到有意义的业务事件。
    • Would you want to expose unfiltered reader observations to your enterprise applications?
      您是否希望将未经过滤的阅读器的观察结果公开给您的企业应用程序？
    • For all but trivial applications, you would probably want these observations to be **processed **further before sending them downstream. 对于所有琐碎的应用程序，您可能希望在将这些观察结果发送到下游之前进一步处理它们。

<2>RFID Event Manager

  • Smoothing filter: Take a series of reader scans and aggregate the results in a way that accounts for the less-than-perfect read accuracy of some RFID technologies.
    平滑滤波器: 对一系列读写器扫描结果进行聚合，以一种方式来考虑某些RFID技术的不完美读取精度。
  • RFID event manager: aggregates raw RF read data from multiple data sources (such as readers) and consolidates and filters it based on previously configured application-level event filters.
    RFID事件管理器: 从多个数据源(如读写器)聚合原始RF读取数据，并根据先前配置的应用程序级事件过滤器对其进行整合和过滤。
  • Most event managers then feed the filtered data to the backend systems.
    大多数事件管理器然后将过滤后的数据提供给后端系统。

<4> Event Filtering and Smoothing System

  • The previous example illustrates the need to:

    • Aggregate observations over time and across multiple readers. 随时间和跨多个读写器聚合观察结果。
    • Filter, consolidate, and transform raw RF reader observations. 过滤、整合和转换原始RF读写器观察结果。

  • Your RFID system needs middleware that runs inside the edges of your enterprise data centers.
    您的RFID系统需要在企业数据中心的边缘运行的中间件。

  • Only important observations are sent to the corporate applications.
    只有重要的观察结果被发送到企业应用程序。

  • The rest of the data is filtered out by the RFID middleware.
    其余的数据由RFID中间件过滤掉。

  • What sorts of things must be filtered out? 必须被过滤掉的是什么样的东西？

    • Overlap of read ranges from antennas in close proximity (two per rack).
      靠近的天线的读取范围重叠（每个货架两个）。
    • As individual scans are less than 100% accurate, these observations need to be aggregated over several read cycles to smooth them out. 由于单独的扫描不到100%准确，因此需要在几个读取周期内对这些观察结果进行聚合以使其平滑化。
    • As customers move along the aisles with products in their hands or in shopping carts, the nearby readers might pick up these items, too. 当顾客手里拿着产品或者推着购物车沿着过道移动时，附近的读写器可能也会捕捉到这些物品。

  • We should filter out any such spurious observations to avoid sending a flood of inaccurate observations to the inventory control system. 我们应该过滤掉任何此类虚假的观测结果，以避免向库存控制系统发送大量不准确的观测结果。

对应五个步骤如下：
  1. The readers pick up raw event data (observations). 读写器捕捉到原始事件数据（观察结果）。
  2. Analyze the raw reader data over several scans and pick up observations based on averages.
    分析几次扫描的原始读写器数据，并根据平均值选择观察结果。
    • Exp: if 70% of observations tell us that an RCA cable box is on a counter, we accept that information.
      例如：如果70%的观察结果告诉我们收银台上有一个RCA电缆盒，我们就接受这个信息。
  3. Filter out duplicate observations from more than one antenna based on relative signal strengths.
    根据相对信号强度过滤掉来自多个天线的重复观察结果。
  4. .
  5. Filter out observations of items moving through the aisles. They generally have lower signal strengths and be transient in nature. 过滤掉沿过道移动的物品的观察结果。它们通常具有较低的信号强度，而且具有瞬态特性。
  6. After the necessary filtering, our data is ready to communicate downstream.
    经过必要的过滤后，我们的数据就可以进行下游通信了。

(6) RFID Service Bus

  • An enterprise service bus (ESB) is a distributed integration platform designed for application connectivity, data

    transformation, guaranteed transactions, and messaging.
    企业服务总线(ESB)是一种分布式集成平台，旨在实现应用程序连接、数据转换、事务保证和消息传递。

  • An RFID service bus is a type of ESB used to integrate applications using RFID data.
    RFID服务总线是一种用于集成使用RFID数据的应用程序的ESB类型。

  • Depending on the implementation you choose, ESB products offer web services, messaging, business process orchestration, and other capabilities. 根据您选择的实现方式，ESB产品提供Web服务、消息传递、业务流程编排和其他功能。

  • Generally, ESBs orchestrate business processes across application, or sometimes enterprise, boundaries.
    一般来说，ESB在应用程序或者有时是企业边界上编排业务流程。

  • RFID systems have to coexist with and extend the capabilities of enterprise applications such as warehouse management systems (WMSs), enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, or point of sale systems.
    RFID系统必须与企业应用程序(如仓库管理系统(WMS)、企业资源规划(ERP)系统或销售点系统)共存，并扩展其功能。

  • Many companies use ESB architectures, which are also referred to as integration servers, to enable disparate applications to collaborate with each other. 许多公司使用ESB架构(也称为集成服务器)来使不同的应用程序能够彼此协作。

    • It provides adapters to understand input coming from these systems, and then it translates that input into a common format (usually XML). 它提供了适配器来理解来自这些系统的输入，然后将该输入转换为通用格式(通常是XML)。
    • Integration servers also provide capabilities that enable you to define and execute business processes.
      集成服务器还提供了使您能够定义和执行业务流程的功能。

  • The primary purpose of an RFID service bus is integrating application-level events captured by event managers into workflows that occur at the enterprise’s edges (edge workflows).
    RFID服务总线的主要目的是将事件管理器捕获的应用程序级事件集成到企业边缘(边缘工作流)中。

  • Specific vendor implementations may vary (some RFID middleware vendors provide scaled-down versions of ESBs), but an RFID service bus is essentially an integration server that runs the relevant edge workflows and provides integration capabilities with modules of enterprise applications such as POS or WMS packages.
    特定供应商的实现可能会有所不同(一些RFID中间件供应商提供了缩减版的ESB)，但RFID服务总线基本上是一个运行相关边缘工作流的集成服务器，并与诸如POS或WMS软件包等企业应用程序的模块提供集成能力。

  • The RFID service bus also integrates into the ESB and can be configured to supply specific events and observations. Because a typical ESB product can be complex and expensive, it’s common to see simpler application server–based custom implementations designed to serve this need. RFID服务总线还集成到ESB中，并可以配置以提供特定的事件和观察结果。由于典型的ESB产品可能复杂且昂贵，因此常见的是看到基于简单应用服务器的定制实现，旨在满足这种需求。

(7) RFID Information Service

  • It is important to realize that an EPC, or for that matter, any other item identification system, is just a unique identifier and by itself does not provide any product details. 重要的是要意识到，EPC，或者说，任何其他物品识别系统，只是一个唯一的标识符，本身并不提供任何产品详细信息。
  • EPCglobal envisions that collaborating businesses and industries will set up a network of EPC Information Service (EPCIS) servers to provide an on-demand repository of information related to individual EPCs.
    EPCglobal设想，合作的企业和行业将建立一个EPC信息服务(EPCIS)服务器网络，以提供与单个EPC相关的信息的按需存储库。
  • Information made available using the EPCIS servers could include the last observed location of an item carrying an EPC (based on an RF reader observation), as well as pricing information and product manuals, if appropriate. 使用EPCIS服务器提供的信息可能包括携带EPC的物品的最后观察到的位置(基于RF读写器观察)，以及价格信息和产品手册(如果合适的话)。

  • An example might be mapping product reference information to its identifier. Even small and non-EPC applications will need to tie the EPC codes to product tracking information (or other reference data related to the product type or instance) and so may need to use and update information managed by an RFID information service. 例如，可能是将产品参考信息映射到其标识符。即使是小型和非EPC应用程序，也需要将EPC代码与产品跟踪信息（或与产品类型或实例相关的其他参考数据）联系起来，因此可能需要使用和更新由RFID信息服务管理的信息。

(1) Privacy and Security 隐私和安全

  • Even though RFID solutions are sometimes offered as enhancements to security, the technology itself presents certain vulnerabilities. 尽管RFID解决方案有时被提供作为安全增强功能，但技术本身存在一定的漏洞。
  • Security considerations for an RFID system permeate various layers of its architecture:
    RFID系统的安全考虑渗透到其架构的各个层次：
    • Ensuring the authenticity of the information stored on the tags themselves.
      • 确保存储在标签上的信息的真实性。
    • Securing the transmission of information between tags and readers.
      • 确保标签和读写器之间信息的传输安全。
    • Ensuring overall application and infrastructure security.
      • 确保整体应用程序和基础设施的安全性。
  • Physical security measures involve efforts to prevent both corruption of tag data and interception of communication between tags and readers. 物理安全措施涉及防止标签数据损坏和拦截标签与读写器之间的通信。
    • Example: A malicious tag reader can attempt to read tags in its vicinity.
      • 例如：恶意的标签读取器可以尝试读取其附近的标签。
  • Security considerations vary depending on business needs. 安全考虑因业务需求而异。
    • Example: Using an RFID-enabled credit card to purchase goods can be done securely if the fulfilment systems on the backend are prepared to handle and detect fraudulent data (as credit card systems are), but a ticketing system that depends on the authenticity of every tag read might be more easily compromised.
      • 例如：如果后端的履行系统准备处理和检测欺诈数据（与信用卡系统一样），那么使用支持RFID的信用卡购买商品是安全的，但是一个依赖每个读取标签的真实性的售票系统可能更容易受到威胁。
  • In addition, individual privacy is a very important consideration in implementing RFID systems and introducing them to operators and users. 此外，个人隐私在实施RFID系统并向操作员和用户介绍它们时是非常重要的考虑因素。

(2) Performance 性能

  • Performance is measured in terms of time taken to perform a unit of activity.

    • 性能是以执行单位活动所需的时间来衡量的。

  • Depending on the layer of the RFID system at which you’re working, the performance considerations will be based on:

    根据您所工作的RFID系统的层次，性能考虑因素将基于：

    • For the physical layer: the time required to recognize a tag, at peak throughput and averaged over a period of time appropriate to system usage. 识别标记所需的时间，在峰值吞吐量和在适合系统使用的一段时间段内平均。

      • We might be tempted to state requirements in the form of scans per second, such as: “Each loading dock must recognize a newly arrived tag within 10 seconds on average at peak periods and 2 seconds on average at nonpeak periods.” 我们可能会倾向于以每秒扫描数的形式陈述需求，例如：“在高峰时段，每个装卸码头平均需要在10秒内识别到新到达的标签，在非高峰时段平均需要2秒内识别到。”

      • Because of the simultaneity of RFID inventories, however, it is much more common (and useful) to state RFID performance requirements in the throughput form, such as “The loading dock must recognize 96 tags per minute on average during peak load periods and 23 tags per minute on average during nonpeak periods.” 然而，由于RFID库存的同时性，通常（并且更有用）以吞吐量的形式陈述RFID性能需求，例如：“在高负载时段，每个装卸码头平均每分钟需要识别96个标签，在非高负载时段平均每分钟需要识别23个标签。”

    • For the RFID event manager and other application-level, components: the time taken to perform a unit of application-level activity. 对于RFID事件管理器和其他应用程序级组件：执行单位应用程序级活动所需的时间。

    • For a tag and ship application, you could define this as the time taken to retrieve an EPC and print it.

      对于标签和发货应用程序，您可以将其定义为检索EPC并打印所需的时间。

(3) Scalability 可扩展性

  • Scalability has to do with how small a system may start and how quickly it must be ready to grow.
    可扩展性与系统的初始规模有关，以及系统必须多快准备好扩展。

  • RFID scalability requirements are often stated like so: “The shipping system should be able to handle 12 loading docks initially and be able to scale to 60 loading docks in 3 years with no more than a 2-week cycle to install a group of 4 new loading docks and bring them online.” RFID可扩展性需求通常如下所述：“装运系统最初应能处理12个装卸码头，并且应能够在3年内扩展到60个装卸码头，每次安装一组4个新的装卸码头并将其上线的周期不超过2周。”

  • However, one of the qualities of RFID systems is the need to plan for site surveys and RF tuning as well as the installation of new equipment and software. 然而，RFID系统的一个特点是需要计划现场调查和射频调谐，以及安装新设备和软件。

    • The RF environment of an area 10 feet from the last install cannot be considered “just the same” as before the install, and there is no guarantee that an earlier site survey of that area is still valid if, for instance, the forklift for certain docks now parks in a different spot when not servicing a load. 10英尺内的最后一个安装点的射频环境不能被视为“与以前相同”，而且不能保证对该区域的早期现场调查仍然有效，例如，如果某些码头的叉车现在停放在不服务装载的不同位置。

  • Once readers are in place, changes to the RF environment can usually be spotted quickly due to changes in performance, but during an install, the environment offers enough variables without adding unexpected signal absorption and reflection.

    一旦读写器就位，由于性能变化，对射频环境的更改通常可以迅速发现，但在安装过程中，环境提供了足够的变量，而无需增加意外的信号吸收和反射。

(4) Manageability 可管理性

  • RFID information servers are typically housed in a data center or server room.

    RFID信息服务器通常存放在数据中心或服务器房间中。

  • Operations staff can monitor and manage the servers using procedures developed for any other application server.

    运维人员可以使用为任何其他应用服务器开发的程序来监控和管理服务器。

  • Event managers and readers are found in loading bays and warehouses, in truck trailers and train yards, and even in corrugated tin shacks in the middle of fields.

    事件管理器和读写器位于装卸区和仓库中，货车拖车和火车场中，甚至在田野中间的瓦楞铁皮小屋中都能找到。

  • A reader may be just as complex as a server, requiring software or firmware upgrades, monitoring, and management. An event manager is likely to be located on server hardware but deployed close to the readers. These are edge systems and must be managed as such. 读写器可能与服务器一样复杂，需要软件或固件升级、监控和管理。事件管理器可能位于服务器硬件上，但部署在靠近读写器的地方。这些是边缘系统，必须像这样进行管理。

    • One of the primary management differences between edge systems and other systems is being: less accessible and more prone to environmental extremes such as heat, dust, and vibration. 边缘系统与其他系统之间的主要管理差异之一是：可访问性较差，更容易受到热、尘埃和振动等环境极端因素的影响。

    • These characteristics of edge systems require both new practices and training for operations personnel and management software capable of managing these relationships. 边缘系统的这些特征需要运维人员采用新的实践方法和培训，并且需要管理软件能够管理这些关系。

  • Manageability requirements are often stated in terms of human resource utilization—for example, “The entire shipping system should be able to operate and meet all availability and performance requirements with only one help desk technician on duty.” 可管理性需求通常以人力资源利用率来陈述，例如：“整个装运系统应该能够在仅有一个服务台技术人员值班的情况下运行并满足所有可用性和性能要求。”

  • Adding sensors introduces additional opportunities for both automation and error.
    添加传感器会为自动化和错误提供额外的机会。

    • If RFID allows you to push a pallet of boxes onto a truck without stopping to scan bar codes manually, it also allows you to push the wrong pallet onto the truck if the right pallet is close enough for the reader to see it. 如果RFID允许您将一托盘箱子推入卡车，而无需手动停下来扫描条形码，那么如果正确的托盘足够靠近读写器，它也允许您将错误的托盘推入卡车。

    • This could be a problem especially if backup readers have powered up to cover a dead spot from a failed reader. Because RFID allows you to automate a process to the point of obviating human interaction, a failed reader could result in serious errors or lost information if there is no equally automated way to monitor the system’s status. 这可能是一个问题，特别是如果备用读写器已经启动以覆盖失败读写器的死区。因为RFID允许您自动化一项过程，以至于不需要人为干预，如果没有同样自动化的方法来监控系统的状态，那么读写器的故障可能会导致严重错误或信息丢失。

    • For now, though, note that real-world RFID deployments will not succeed without automated provisioning, monitoring, and management of the RFID infrastructure. 但是，需要注意的是，现实世界中的RFID部署如果没有对RFID基础设施进行自动化的配置、监控和管理，是不会成功的。

(5) Extensibility and Maintainability 可扩展性和可维护性

  • For most organizations, RFID readers are the first step into a new space at the edge.
    对于大多数组织来说，RFID读写器是进入边缘新空间的第一步。

  • Some industries—especially within the areas of manufacturing, agriculture, and defense—have worked with distributed sensors for some time, but a company new to RFID may falsely assume that once it gets the RFID readers talking to the management applications, it’s done. 一些行业，特别是在制造业、农业和国防领域，已经与分布式传感器一起工作了一段时间，但是对于刚开始接触RFID的公司来说，可能会错误地认为一旦它让RFID读写器与管理应用程序交流，就完成了。

  • Mandates and contracts that today require RFID compliance could easily require temperature, humidity, shock, or magnetic field exposure tracking tomorrow. 如今需要RFID合规的法规和合同明天很可能会要求温度、湿度、震动或磁场暴露跟踪。

  • RFID systems are in a state of flux as technology advances and standards only begin to solidify.
    随着技术的进步和标准的开始巩固，RFID系统处于不断变化的状态。

  • An RFID installation of any size must be prepared to plug in readers with different mounting requirements, different management interfaces, and even different manufacturers depending on what is available this week.

    任何规模的RFID安装都必须准备好根据本周的可用情况连接具有不同安装要求、不同管理界面甚至不同制造商的读写器。

  • Requirements in this area are often stated thus: 在这个领域的要求通常如下所述：

    • “The receiving system should be able to use any EPC Class I reader from any vendor, with less than three person-days of configuration for a new type.” 接收系统应该能够使用任何供应商的任何EPCI类阅读器，新型的配置少于3人天。”

8. ARCHITECTURE GUIDELINES

  • The infrastructure for RFID edge components must be incredibly robust. RFID边缘组件的基础设施必须非常强大。

    • Ideally, the readers, RFID middleware, and so on should support effortless plug-and-play functioning.

      理想情况下，读写器、RFID中间件等应支持轻松的即插即用功能。

    • You need to employ automated means to monitor and manage RFID readers.

      您需要采用自动化手段来监控和管理RFID读写器。

    • Also, you’ll want to shield your applications from the large number of readers deployed in a store or other location.

      同时，您还需要保护您的应用程序免受在商店或其他位置部署的大量读写器的影响。

  • To support all this, your edge architectures will need to be more flexible, scalable, robust, secure, and manageable than ever before. 为了支持所有这些，您的边缘架构需要比以往任何时候都更加灵活、可扩展、强大、安全和可管理。

  • As is always the case in a technology’s early adoption phase, RFID standards and products are rapidly evolving.

    正如在技术早期采用阶段总是如此，RFID标准和产品正在快速发展。

  • Event managers based on standards such as ALE will have to coexist with other vendor-specific extensions to vertical applications such as point-of-sale and warehouse-management systems. 基于ALE等标准的事件管理器将不得不与其他供应商特定的扩展共存，这些扩展是垂直应用程序的一部分，如销售点和仓库管理系统。

  • When developing your RFID architecture roadmap, it is very important to take a close look at both your business and systemic quality requirements and how they might evolve over time. 在制定RFID架构路线图时，非常重要的是密切关注您的业务和系统质量需求以及它们随时间的演变情况。

(1) Begin with Business Requirements 从业务需求开始

  • The most overlooked aspect when designing RFID systems is business processes.

    在设计RFID系统时最容易被忽视的方面是业务流程。

  • People often forget that technology’s ultimate purpose is to solve business problems.

    人们经常忘记技术的最终目的是解决业务问题。

  • This is the case even more so with promising new technologies, as the newness and buzz surrounding these technologies drive a bottom-up way of thinking.

    这在涉及前景广阔的新技术时尤其如此，因为这些技术的新颖性和激情推动了自下而上的思考方式。

  • While you’re defining the business requirements and capturing functional requirements, pay careful attention to accounting for exception processing. 在定义业务需求和捕获功能需求时，务必注意考虑异常处理。

    • These are the “what if” situations, such as what to do if a reader goes down, or a tag falls off a package, or you are not able to read a particular tag. 这些是“如果发生什么”情况，比如如果读写器出现故障、标签从包裹上掉下来，或者无法读取特定的标签时该怎么办。

  • The exception scenarios should provide capabilities to use alternate means to accomplish the task.

    异常情况应该提供使用替代手段来完成任务的能力。

    • For example, if a read registers only 50 packages of a product when an operator visually determines that the number should be closer to 100, you could create alternate workflows to obtain the inventory count manually. 例如，如果操作员通过目测确定应该接近100个，但读取仅显示了50个产品包装，则可以创建替代工作流程手动获取库存计数。

(2) Don’t Forget Your Existing Infrastructure 不要忘记您现有的基础设施

  • More often than not, RFID systems will have to coexist with bar code systems, providing alternate, more automated capabilities. 很多时候，RFID系统将不得不与条形码系统共存，提供替代的、更自动化的功能。
  • When planning the architecture of your RFID system, pay careful attention to your existing systems and business processes. 在规划RFID系统的架构时，要特别注意您现有的系统和业务流程。

(3) Process Data at the Edge Where Possible 在可能的情况下在边缘处理数据

  • Process as much data as possible at its source. This means that the raw RFID observations collected by readers should be filtered at the edge before sending only what is meaningful to applications. 尽可能在数据源处处理尽可能多的数据。这意味着读写器收集到的原始RFID观察结果应在边缘进行过滤，然后只将对应用程序有意义的数据发送出去。

  • Develop a data-management architecture that keeps data needed for the workflow at the edge on the edge.

    开发一个数据管理架构，将工作流程所需的数据保留在边缘。

  • If RFID data gives you enough information to sort items at the factory into different bins based on their models, then don’t make that decision at corporate headquarters. 如果RFID数据为您提供了足够的信息，可以根据型号将工厂的物品分类到不同的箱子中，那么就不要在公司总部做出该决定。

  • Have the edge server make the call based on an understanding of which item types go in which bins.

    让边缘服务器根据了解哪些物品类型应该放在哪些箱子中来做出决定。

  • The list of which item types go in which bins can be updated from time to time via an automated process.

    可以通过自动化流程不时更新哪些物品类型放在哪些箱子中的列表。

(4) Track Items to the Level Your Business Processes Will Support 根据您的业务流程支持程度跟踪物品

  • The item-level tracking ability offered by RFID systems can come in handy for many applications. You might wonder then why we are listing it as a challenge.

    RFID系统提供的物品级跟踪能力对许多应用非常有用。那么您可能会想为什么我们将其列为一项挑战。

  • The real challenge is not in identifying an RF tag uniquely, but in figuring out what you are going to do with that information.

    真正的挑战不在于唯一地识别RF标签，而在于弄清楚您打算如何处理这些信息。

  • More information is not always better. Weigh the cost of generating, storing, and processing this information against the business benefits. 更多信息并不总是更好。要权衡产生、存储和处理这些信息的成本与业务收益之间的关系。

  • Tracking items at instance level greatly increases the data that is generated as the items move through the supply chain.

    在实例级别跟踪物品会大大增加随着物品在供应链中移动而生成的数据量。

  • In addition to adjusting to the increased volume of information, this finer-grained tracking data would need to be incorporated into the business processes and thus the systems that support them.

    除了调整到信息量的增加之外，这种更精细的跟踪数据还需要纳入业务流程以及支持它们的系统中。

9. SYSTEM MANAGEMENT

  • The level and scale of automation for some RFID systems poses significant challenges for system administrators and managers. 对于一些RFID系统，自动化程度和规模的提升给系统管理员和经理们带来了重大挑战。

  • As stated earlier, if a manual bar code scanner is down, a human operator will immediately pick up on it and call for service. The automated processing capabilities that RFID technologies offer make automated detection of system faults and stoppages indispensable. Similarly, we need to provide novel solutions for provisioning, monitoring, and managing our silently ticking antennas, readers, and other RFID infrastructure components. 正如前面所述，如果手动条形码扫描仪出现故障，人类操作员会立即注意到并要求维修。RFID技术提供的自动处理能力使得系统故障和停机的自动检测变得不可或缺。同样，我们需要提供新颖的解决方案，以提供我们悄悄运行的天线、读写器和其他RFID基础设施组件的配置、监视和管理。

  • Think ease of configuring, provisioning, managing, and monitoring when deciding on infrastructure components such as readers, sensors, event managers, servers, storage, and networks.

    在决定读写器、传感器、事件管理器、服务器、存储和网络等基础设施组件时，要考虑配置、供应、管理和监视的便利性。

  • Build redundancy into the architecture. Have a plan in place for what should happen when an antenna, a reader, or an event manager malfunctions. 在架构中建立冗余性。在天线、读写器或事件管理器发生故障时，制定应对计划。

  • Consider using RFID middleware and other system management solutions to stay ahead of the difficulties that come with a fast-growing edge infrastructure. 考虑使用RFID中间件和其他系统管理解决方案，以应对快速增长的边缘基础设施带来的困难。

(1) Start with Architecture 从架构开始

  • Much of the time, enterprise architecture grows out of its selection of products.

    大多数情况下，企业架构是根据产品的选择而发展的。

  • With minor exceptions, over time, a proprietary architecture that is locked into a particular product and vendor develops.

    有少量例外情况，但随着时间的推移，会发展出与特定产品和供应商锁定的专有架构。

  • Begin with well-defined interfaces between different layers, such as event managers, applications, and integration servers, and then choose products that fit your architecture. Later, this will pay in greater flexibility and independence.

    从不同层之间的明确定义的接口开始，例如事件管理器、应用程序和集成服务器，然后选择符合您架构的产品。以后，这将带来更大的灵活性和独立性。

(2) Use RFID Middleware 使用RFID中间件

  • It is necessary to use RFID middleware for all but trivial implementations.

    除了微不足道的实现外，使用RFID中间件是必要的。

  • RFID middleware helps decouple your enterprise applications from the physical architecture of your RFID systems; those applications don’t need to know about reader vendors, their APIs, or the physical configuration of readers and antennas. RFID中间件有助于将企业应用程序与RFID系统的物理架构分离开来；这些应用程序不需要了解读写器供应商、它们的API或读写器和天线的物理配置。

  • RFID middleware provides the important function of filtering the highly fragmented data that comes from the readers. By cutting down the volume of data that passes over wide area networks, event filtering also allows you to define application-level events and pass more meaningful information to your enterprise applications.

    RFID中间件提供了过滤来自读写器的高度碎片化数据的重要功能。通过减少通过广域网传输的数据量，事件过滤还允许您定义应用程序级事件，并将更有意义的信息传递给您的企业应用程序。

(3) Reader’s Operational Specifications 读写器的操作规范

  • To make reader’s performance meets the requirements of the application, certain operational specifications should be followed while using the reader. 为了使读写器的性能符合应用需求，使用读写器时应遵循某些操作规格

<1> 影响识别范围因素

  • R&W range (read out and write in range) is one of the key performance metrics that affect the scope of reader applications. Influencing factors: R&W范围（读取和写入范围）是影响读写器应用范围的关键性能指标之一。影响因素包括：

    • The way that antenna is coupled 天线耦合的方式
    • The output power of the reader’s RF signal 读写器的射频信号的输出功率
    • The frequency of RF carrier signal 射频载波信号的频率

  • Antenna direction Antenna方向

    • When the polarization direction of the reader antenna and tag antenna match, the recognition range becomes maximum. In practice, mismatch is unavoidable, where measures as followed can be applied: 当读写器天线和标签天线的极化方向相匹配时，识别范围最大。在实际应用中，无法避免不匹配的情况，可以采取以下措施：
      • Change the direction of the reader antenna 改变读取器天线的方向
      • Use multiple redundant antennas 使用多个冗余天线
      • Increase the power of the reader antenna 增加读取器天线的功率

      生命周期应用，如维护历史，需要随时间添加或修改有关物品的数据。

I. Passive Tags

  • No power supply: Electrical current transmitted by the RFID reader inductively powers the tag, allowing the tag to transmit its information back. 无电源供应：电流通过RFID读取器感应地供电给标签，使标签能够将其信息传回。

  • Limited range of broadcast: Short distance, a few meters. 有限的广播范围：距离有限，几米之内。

  • Limited transmission capability: Typically, no more than an ID number. 有限的传输能力：通常不超过一个ID号码。

  • Ideal for places preventing battery replacement. 适用于不易更换电池的场所。

    ❖ Applications: include inventory, product shipping and tracking, hospitals and other medical purposes, and antitheft, can be used implanted into the human body or placed under the skin. 应用程序：包括库存、产品运输和跟踪、医院和其他医疗目的，以及防盗功能，可用于植入人体或放置在皮肤下。

  • Tags consist of a silicon device (chip) and antenna circuit. 标签由硅器件（芯片）和天线电路组成。

    • Purpose of antenna circuit: Induces an energizing signal and sends a modulated RF signal. 天线电路的目的：诱导一个激励信号并发送调制的射频信号。
    • Read range: Largely depends upon the antenna circuit and size. 读取范围：主要取决于天线电路和尺寸。

  • The antenna circuit is made of a LC resonant circuit or E-field dipole antenna, depending on the carrier frequency.
    天线电路由LC谐振电路或E场偶极天线组成，取决于载波频率。

    • The LC resonant circuit is used for frequencies of less than 100 MHz. At these frequencies, the communication between the reader and tag is achieved with magnetic coupling between the two antennas. An antenna utilizing inductive coupling is often called a magnetic dipole antenna LC谐振电路用于频率小于100 MHz。在这些频率下，读取器和标签之间的通信是通过两个天线之间的磁耦合来实现的。利用电感耦合的天线通常被称为磁偶极子天线。

  • The antenna circuits must be designed to maximize the magnetic coupling between them:
    天线电路的设计必须能最大限度地提高它们之间的磁耦合：

    • The LC circuit: tuned to carrier frequency of the reader. LC电路：调谐到读卡器的载波频率。
    • The Q of the tuned circuit must be maximized. 调谐电路的Q必须最大化。
    • The antenna size must be maximized within physical limit of application requirement. 天线尺寸必须在应用要求的物理极限范围内最大。

Backscattering for Passive Tags

  • **Backscattering **of the carrier frequency for sending data from tag to reader.
    将数据从标签发送到阅读器的载波频率的后向散射。

    • The amplitude of backscattering signal is modulated with the tag’s data.
      利用标签的数据对后向散射信号的振幅进行了调制。
    • Modulation data: ASK (NRZ or Manchester), FSK, or PSK.

  • During backscatter modulation, the incoming RF carrier signal to the tag is loaded and unloaded, causing amplitude modulation of the carrier, corresponding to the tag data bits. 在背散射调制期间，发射到标签的入射射频载波信号被装载和卸载，导致载波的振幅调制，与标签数据位相对应。

  • The RF voltage induced in the tag’s antenna is amplitude modulated by the modulation signal (data) of the tag device. 标签天线感应的射频电压被标签设备的调制信号（数据）进行幅度调制。

  • Amplitude modulation can be achieved by using a modulation transistor across the LC resonant circuit or partially across resonant circuit. 可以通过在LC谐振电路上使用调制晶体管或部分跨越谐振电路来实现幅度调制。

  • Changes in the voltage amplitude of the tag’s antenna can affect the voltage of the reader antenna. 标签天线电压振幅的变化可以影响读取器天线的电压。

    • By monitoring the changes in the reader antenna voltage (due to the tag’s modulation data), the data in the tag can be reconstructed. 通过监控读取器天线电压的变化（由于标签的调制数据），可以重构标签中的数据。

    • The RF voltage link between the reader and tag antennas are often compared to weakly coupled transformer coils; as the secondary winding, tag coil, is momentarily shunted, the primary winding, reader coil, experiences a momentary voltage change. 读取器和标签天线之间的射频电压链接通常与弱耦合的变压器线圈相比较；由于次级绕组，即标签线圈，被暂时分流，主绕组，即读取器线圈，经历了瞬时电压变化。

II. Active Tags

  • Active tags, also called transponders: 主动标签，也称为无线电标签：

    • Powered by on-board power source: Usually a battery about the size of a coin. It does not require inductions to provide current, as is true of the passive tags.
      由板载电源供电：通常是硬币大小的电池。与被动标签不同，它不需要感应提供电流。

    • Longer transmission distance: 30 m, up to over 100 m. 较长的传输距离：30 米，甚至超过 100 米。

    • Designed with a variety of specialized electronics: Including microprocessors, different types of sensors, or I/O devices. Depending on the target function of the tag, this information can be processed and stored for immediate or later retrieval by a reader. 设计有各种专用电子设备：包括微处理器、不同类型的传感器或 I/O 设备。根据标签的目标功能，这些信息可以被处理和存储，以供读取器立即或以后检索。

    • Larger and more expensive: Than passive RFID tags. The additional space taken up by a battery.

      更大更昂贵：比被动 RFID 标签。电池占用了额外的空间。

    • Hold more data about the product: Commonly used for high-value asset tracking. A feature that most active tags have, and most passive tags do not, is the ability to store data received from a transceiver. 保存更多产品数据：通常用于高价值资产跟踪。大多数主动标签具有的一个功能，而大多数被动标签没有的功能是能够存储从收发器接收的数据。

    • Ideal in environments with electromagnetic interference: Since they can broadcast a stronger signal in situations that require a greater distance between the tag and the transmitter. 在电磁干扰环境中表现出色：因为它们可以在需要标签与发射器之间更大距离的情况下广播更强的信号。

    • The information in the memory can be reprogrammed by interrogator command.
      内存中的信息可以通过询问者命令重新编程。

    • Active tags can spontaneously transmit an ID. There are various types of unscheduled transmission type, such as when there are changes in vibration or temperature. 主动标签可以自动发送 ID。有各种类型的非预定传输类型，例如当振动或温度发生变化时。

  • A semiactive or semipassive tag (naming convention depends on the manufacturer) also has an on-board battery.

    半主动或半被动标签（命名约定取决于制造商）也有板载电池。

    • The battery in this case is only used to operate the chip. 本例中的电池仅用于操作芯片。
    • Like the passive tag, it uses the energy in the electromagnetic field to wake up thechip and to transmit the data to the reader. 和被动标签一样，它利用电磁场中的能量来唤醒芯片，并将数据传输给阅读器。
    • These tags are sometimes called battery-assisted passive (BAP) 这些标签有时被称为电池辅助被动式（BAP）

  • Active Tag Classification 通常可以识别出两种类型的主动标签系统

  1. Wake-up tag systems: Are deactivated, or asleep, until activated by a coded message from a reader or interrogator.

    唤醒标签系统：直到由读取器或询问者发送的编码消息激活之前，标签处于停用或休眠状态。

    • In the sleep mode, limiting the current drain to a low-level alert function conserves the battery energy.
      • 在睡眠模式下，通过限制电流流失到低级警报功能来节省电池能量。
    • Where larger memories are accommodated, there is also generally a need to access data on an object or internal file basis to avoid having to transfer the entire amount of data so held.
      • 如果有较大的内存，通常还需要根据对象或内部文件的基础来访问数据，以避免必须传输所有所持有的数据。
    • Applications: toll payment collection, checkpoint control, and in tracking cargo.
      • 应用：收费支付、检查点控制以及货物跟踪。

  2. Awake tag or beacon systems: Are responsive to interrogation without a coded message being required to switch the tag from an energy conservation mode. 唤醒标记或信标系统：在不需要编码消息的情况下对询问作出响应，不需要编码消息即可将标签从能量节约模式切换出来。

    • Because greater switching rate is generally associated with higher energy usage, these tags generally operate at a lower data transfer rates and memory sizes than wake-up tags, so they conserve battery energy in this way.
      • 由于更高的切换速率通常与更高的能量使用相关联，因此这些标签通常以较低的数据传输速率和内存大小运行，所以以这种方式节省电池能量。
    • This type of tag is the most widely used of the two, and because of lower component costs it is generally less expensive than wake-up tag systems.
      • 这种类型的标签是两种类型中最广泛使用的，并且由于组件成本较低，通常比唤醒标签系统便宜。
    • Applications: most real-time locating systems (RTLS), where the precise location of an asset needs to be tracked.     RFID标签需要能量来感知、计算和通信，这进一步分为三类：

    • Storage (储存): batteries, capacitors. 电池、电容器。

    • Energy harvesting mechanisms (能量收集机制): vibrations/movement, photovoltaic, thermal gradient, and so on.

      振动/运动、光伏、温差梯度等。

    • Energy transfer (能量传输): inductive coupling, capacitive coupling, backscatter. 电感耦合、电容耦合、后向散射。

  • Because many of these devices are expected to operate with minimum of human intervention, optimizing power consumption is a very important research area.
    因为许多这些设备预计需要尽量少的人为干预，优化功耗是一个非常重要的研究领域。

  • RFID tags may derive the energy to operate either from an on-tag battery or by scavenging power from the electromagnetic radiation emitted by tag readers. RFID标签可以从标签上的电池获取操作所需的能量，也可以通过从标签读取器发射的电磁辐射中回收能量来运行。

  • Storage refers to the way devices store power for their operation done either by using batteries or by using capacitors.

    储存是指设备通过使用电池或电容器来存储其运行所需的电力的方式。

  • Batteries are used when a longer life is required, and capacitors are used in applications that require energy bursts for very short durations. 当需要更长的寿命时使用电池，而在需要非常短时间内能量爆发的应用中使用电容器。

(2) Power Harvesting Systems电力收集系统

  • Power harvesting (sometimes termed energy scavenging) is the process of acquiring energy from the surrounding environment (ambient energy) and converting it into usable electrical energy. 动力收集（有时称为能量捕捉）是从周围环境（环境能量）获取能量并将其转化为可用电能的过程。

    • Exp (例子): the self-winding watch cleverly extracting mechanical energy from the wearer’s arm movements.

      自动上链手表巧妙地从佩戴者的手臂运动中提取机械能。

  • The energy transfer is the way by which passive RF devices are powered. 能量传输是被动射频设备供电的方式。

    1. Inductive coupling (电感耦合): is the transfer of energy between two electronic circuits due to the mutual inductance between the two circuits. 是由于两个电子电路之间的互感而进行能量传输的过程。

    2. Capacitive coupling (电容耦合): is the transfer of energy between two circuits due to the mutual capacitance between the two circuits. 是由于两个电路之间的互电容而进行能量传输的过程。

    3. Passive backscattering (被动背散射): is a way of reflecting back the energy from one circuit to another. 是将能量从一个电路反射回另一个电路的一种方式。

  • Passive tag is powered through inductive coupling or far-field energy harvesting. 被动标签通过电感耦合或远场能量收集来供电。

(3) Active Power Sources 主动电源

  • Battery-assisted backscatter tags have their own power source to preenergize the silicon chip.

    电池辅助背散射标签具有自己的电源来预先为硅芯片充能。

  • Lithium batteries offer a set of performance and safety characteristics optimal for RFID tag applications.

    锂电池为RFID标签应用提供了一组性能和安全特性，最适合RFID标签应用。

  • Two types of batteries that are most common are: 最常见的两种电池类型是：

<1> Primary lithium/manganese dioxide (Li-MnO2): relatively safe compared to LiSOCL2, does not develop any gas or pressure during battery operation, cannot operate at voltages greater than 3V. 主要锂/二氧化锰（Li-MnO2）电池：与LiSOCL2相比相对安全，电池运行过程中不会产生任何气体或压力，不能在大于3V的电压下工作。

  • At least two Li-MnO2 cells must be connected in series to ensure a proper margin of safety for system reliability.

    为了确保系统可靠性的适当安全边界，至少必须连接两个Li-MnO2电池。

  • This requirement adds weight and cost while potentially decreasing reliability due to increased part count.

    该要求增加了重量和成本，同时由于零件数量增加，可能会降低可靠性。

  • Suited for applications that have relatively high continuous or pulse current requirements.

    适用于具有相对高连续或脉冲电流需求的应用。

<2> Lithium-thionyl chloride (Li-SOCL2): It has the highest open-circuit voltage of 3.6V. 硫酰氯化锂（Li-SOCL2）电池：其开路电压最高可达3.6V。

  • For most applications, only one cell of Li-SOCL 2 is required to maintain sufficient operating voltage. 对于大多数应用程序，只需要一个Li-SOCL 2 电池即可保持足够的工作电压。

  • Suited for applications that have relatively high continuous or pulse current requirements.

    适用于具有相对高连续或脉冲电流需求的应用。

(4) Other Power Sources其他电源

  • Power generation from the user may alleviate such design restrictions and may enable new products such as batteryless on-body sensors. 用户产生的电力可能会缓解这种设计限制，并可能使新产品如无电池的体内传感器成为可能。
  • Power may be recovered passively from body heat, arm motion, typing, and walking or actively through user actions such as winding or pedaling. 电力可以 passively 从身体热量、手臂运动、打字和行走中 passively 回收，或者通过用户 winding 或 pedaling 等主动行为进行回收。
  • Nuclear power: revealed as possible source of power for wireless sensor and RFID networks. 核能：被揭示为无线传感器和RFID网络的可能电源。
    • It produces huge amount of energy: the energy density measured in mWh/mg is 0.3 for a lithium-ion battery, 3 for a methanol-based fuel cell, 850 for a tritium-based nuclear battery, and 57,000 for a polonium-210 nuclear battery. 它产生大量能量：以mWh/mg计量的能量密度为0.3对于锂离子电池、3对于基于甲醇的燃料电池、850对于基于氚的核电池，以及57,000对于210钋核电池。
    • The current efficiency of a nuclear battery is around 4%, and current research projects (e.g., as part of the new DARPA program called Radio-Isotope Micropower Sources) aim at 20%. 核电池的当前效率约为4％，当前的研究项目（例如，作为名为Radio-Isotope Micropower Sources的新DARPA项目的一部分）的目标是20％。
  • A novel power supply for implantable biosensors is near infrared light (NIR) transmission which recharges a lithium secondary battery wirelessly through the skin. 植入式生物传感器的一种新型电源是近红外光（NIR）传输，通过皮肤无线充电锂二次电池。
  • The Sun is a good source of NIR light, and its use requires no other external device to deliver energy to the recharging system. 太阳是近红外光的良好来源，其使用不需要其他外部设备将能量输送到充电系统。
  • A photovoltaic cell array and the rectifier using Schottky diodes implanted beneath the skin can receive NIR light through the skin and charge the battery that is directly powering an implanted biosensor(s). 植入在皮肤下的太阳能电池阵列和使用施特基二极管的整流器可以通过皮肤接收近红外光并为直接为植入式生物传感器供电的电池充电。
  • In 2008, a U.K.-based consortium of companies has successfully designed and clinically tested an in-body microgenerator that converts energy from the heartbeat into power for implanted medical devices. 2008年，总部位于英国的一家公司联合体成功设计并临床测试了一种体内微发电机，将心跳产生的能量转化为植入医疗设备的电力。
  • The microgenerator could help power implanted medical devices by augmenting the existing battery for devices such as cardiac pacemakers and implanted defibrillators. 该微发电机可以通过增加已有的电池为心脏起搏器和植入式除颤器等设备提供电力。
  • In preclinical testing, the microgenerator successfully produced one-third of the energy required to power a conventional cardiac pacemaker. 在临床前测试中，微发电机成功产生了传统心脏起搏器所需能量的三分之一。
